Procedural Salamander AI

2D AI Simulation · Three-Person Team

Procedural Animation · Inverse Kinematics · Reactive Agents

Role: Team Member · Engine: Raylib (C++) · Scope: AI/Animation · Year: 2023/24

Overview

This project is a 2D simulation built in C++ with Raylib by a three-person team. Inspired by procedural animation in games such as Rain World, our goal was to explore how inverse kinematics, procedural motion, and simple AI agents could generate lifelike and emergent behaviours.

The simulation features a salamander that roams its environment, animated procedurally rather than with sprites. It uses IK for limbs, a spring-mass system for the tail, and a field-of-view perception system to detect and react to flies. The flies themselves follow randomized patterns and flee when approached, producing dynamic interactions.

Key Features

We collaborated equally on all aspects of the project. These are the main highlights:

Inverse Kinematics for Limbs

Implemented custom 2D IK algorithms to animate the salamander’s legs without keyframes, ensuring smooth, realistic poses and avoiding unnatural overlaps.

Procedural Tail and Body Motion

Built a spring-mass physics system to simulate the salamander’s tail and freeform movement, giving the creature a fluid, snake-like feel.

Reactive Fly Behaviour

Flies move with randomized AI and flee when the salamander approaches its field of view, producing emergent predator-prey interactions.

Takeaways

Building this simulation deepened my understanding of procedural animation and AI-driven behaviours. Developing our own inverse kinematics and physics systems showed how expressive math-driven motion can be, and highlighted the value of collaboration in a small team. More than a technical exercise, it reinforced how creativity and constraints can combine to produce believable and engaging simulations.
